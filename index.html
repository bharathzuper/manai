Project Overview

Design Brief

Not a museum, not a show home —
a house that feels genuinely rooted in its culture
and still completely liveable today.

— Core Design Intent

Manai is a private residence on an 880 sq.ft plot in Old Washermanpet, Chennai. The structure is G+2 with the foundation built to support G+3 for future expansion. Total built-up area is approximately 2,772 sq.ft. The ground floor handles parking and servant quarters. The first floor is my parents' living space — their bedrooms, living and dining area, kitchen, and everything they need for comfortable daily life. The second floor is my personal space — designed around how I want to live, work, and spend my time at home.

The aesthetic we are going for is Neo-Deccan Heritage: traditional South Indian design sensibility brought into a modern home. Not a museum, not a show home — a house that feels genuinely rooted in its culture and still completely liveable today.

Art is a core requirement throughout the house, not an afterthought. We want traditional paintings, handcrafted elements, and artisan work integrated into the architecture at every level — on walls, at entrances, in corridors, not just in one feature room. The house should also feel green and alive — plants are a serious part of the design, indoors and wherever outdoor space allows. The overall feeling we are after is a home that is rich in character, full of craft, and still feels fresh and breathing — not heavy or dark.

The design team should treat every surface as an opportunity, not just the obvious ones. Please review the floor plans, structural drawings, and reference images in this document.

Floor-by-Floor

Requirements per Floor

01

Ground Floor

Parking & Services

Requirements: Covered car parking, a servant room with attached toilet, a lift shaft (4' × 5') designed to accommodate a home lift, main staircase (6' × 14'4"), and an OTS (6' × 3'). The entrance should have a heritage-style threshold — consider a Mughal arch doorway with carved teak frame. Even though this is the service floor, we want the staircase to make a visual statement from the ground level itself. Flooring should be durable but not generic — consider Athangudi tiles or terracotta.

Requirements: Living + Dining area (9'4" × 17'1") — this is the main gathering space and needs a stained glass ceiling feature that casts colored light. Kitchen (10' × 8') — should be functional with modern fittings but heritage aesthetics (patterned tiles, brass fixtures). Bedroom 2 (10' × 10'4") and Bedroom 3 (9'6" × 9'4") — both need carved teak window frames and jali screens for ventilation. Two attached toilets with zellige tile work. Sit-out (9'7" × 10') — this is important, needs to feel like a verandah with carved teak railings and space for morning seating. All rooms need peacock motif integration in either ceiling, doorframe, or floor pattern.

02

First Floor

Family Living

03

Second Floor

Private Suite

Requirements: Master Bedroom (9'9" × 14'3") — needs to be the most designed room in the house with a feature wall (3D relief mural or carved teak headboard wall). Lounge area (13' × 9'4") — this will serve as both a screening/cinema room and a hi-fi music listening space, needs acoustic consideration and mood lighting. Dressing room (9'9" × 7'7"). Toilet (5'10" × 7'9") — we want black Kadappa stone flooring with zellige tile walls, this should feel like a spa. Patio — open to sky. Sit-out (9'6" × 9'4"). Workstation (6'4" × 4'3"). We also need a dedicated Pooja room — turmeric yellow and white marble, with a small Mughal arch niche. This floor should have the heaviest design treatment.

This is our broad colour direction — Chettinad & eco-heritage inspired. Creme Ivory as the neutral base (lime wash walls), with terracotta, indigo, green, and gold as accents placed strategically. Oxide red for Chettinad tile flooring and accents. The design team can adjust shades but should stay within this warm, earthy, heritage family. No cool greys, no stark whites, no synthetic-feeling colours.
